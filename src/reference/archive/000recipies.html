Squirrel Scoops

A crunchy, nut-packed treat bursting with forest flavors! Handcrafted with the finest selection of woodland treasures, these Squirrel Scoops are a chewy-crunchy snack designed to tantalize even the most discerning squirrel palate. Each bite-sized treat is packed with a delicious blend of roasted hazelnuts, crushed acorns, sunflower seeds, pumpkin seeds, and a touch of dried apple, all lightly bound with natural tree sap syrup for a sweet, sticky finish. Wrapped in a delicate shell of toasted bark flakes and rolled in a dusting of ground pine nuts, this snack not only satisfies hunger but delivers a burst of energy for midday tree-leaping adventures. Perfect for hoarding or sharing with your bushy-tailed buddies!

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup crushed acorns (leached to remove tannins)
  • 1/4 cup roasted hazelnuts, chopped
  • 2 tbsp crushed walnuts
  • 2 tbsp sunflower seeds (unsalted)
  • 2 tbsp pumpkin seeds (raw or roasted)
  • 1 tbsp rolled oats (optional, for added texture)
  • 2 tbsp dried apple, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp dried cranberries or raisins (unsweetened)
  • 2 tbsp natural tree sap syrup (or substitute with maple syrup or honey)
  • 1 tbsp nut butter (e.g., almond or sunflower seed butter)
  • 1 tbsp toasted bark flakes (bark like cinnamon or birch bark shavings, finely crumbled)
  • 1 tbsp ground pine nuts or finely ground sunflower seeds for rolling

Instructions

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine: Acorns (make sure they’ve been properly leached to remove tannins!), hazelnuts, walnuts, sunflower seeds, and pumpkin seeds - Rolled oats (if using) - Finely chopped dried apple and cranberries/raisins.
  2. Stir in: Nut butter and tree sap syrup. Mix until the ingredients are sticky and clump together. Add a bit more nut butter or syrup if it feels too dry.
  3. Shape Treats: Take small scoops (about 1 tablespoon) and roll into tight little balls or press into bite-sized discs.
  4. On a plate, mix: Ground pine nuts and toasted bark flakes. Then roll each treat gently in the coating until evenly covered.
  5. Let Set: Place treats on a parchment-lined tray and refrigerate for 30–60 minutes to firm up.
